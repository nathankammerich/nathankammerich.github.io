Highschool

In highschool, I was able to take career center classes to try and figure out what I wanted to do with my career. I’ve always been fascinated in technology and what can be achieved, and this love was furthered in the coding classes that I took at the career center. I received credit for programming I and II at college, and learned a lot about C++/good coding practices, which proved to be very helpful in college. I also took some classes centered around digital media and use of the adobe suite. This is what inspired me to pursue a minor in digital storytelling to complement the IT major.

Early College

Early college was also a time of exploration. I was set on IT at this point, but since the field is so broad with so many potential career options, I was trying to make my way through all of these gen-ed requirements so I could focus more on classes for my major. I got most of these out of the way in the first couple years, which gave me time to adjust to college. The first year was all online due to COVID-19, but afterwards I was able to take some classes in person, which was helpful for me.

Late College

In the past year or so ago, I’ve had to start narrowing down my preferences. I think I have identified digital media work to be my strong suit, and I am also very interested in a career in web design/development, or UX design. My plan is to work an internship over the summer to further identify what kind of job I want, and gain some real work experience.

Job

My time at Hy-Vee over the past 6 years has taught me so much about how to be a good worker, a good person, a good co-worker, and more. Spending this much time at this job meant I learned a lot of different skills and became very easy to adapt to new environments and tasks. I think my communication skills have grown a ton, and my work ethic has also stayed strong during the 6 years.
